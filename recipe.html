About

My favourite meal/best food (bf like I do call it) is Jollof Rice with any available common protein. I mentioned common before the protein because I don't want someone feeding me unusual proteins in the name of food (so yea, our focus is sumptuous Jollof Rice). Smoky is extra spec.

Jollof Rice, also known as benachin and thieboudienne, is a one-pot meal that originated from the Wolof tribe in the 14th century. The Wolof Empire, ruling parts of modern-day Senegal, The Gambia and Mauritania, popularized jollof throughout West Africa. I never really knew about at all that up until now, maybe because I never cared to find out; but now we know all thanks to Google Doodle. One thing I'm aware of is the Jollof Wars, Nigerians and Ghanaians obsessing over who makes the best jollof. I really don't care about that too, just make my bf nice and yummy. Although I recently fell in love with the basmati Jollof and I read that's kinda a Ghanaian recipe (this is not me cheating on my Nigerian Jollof *tongue out*).

Let's prep the main dish.

Ingredients

Quantity
Rice 2 cups
Tomato puree 2 satchets
Pepper blend As desired
Onions 2 medium size
Chicken/beef stock 2 cups
Curry 1 tsp
Thyme 1 tsp
Bouillon cubes 2 cubes
Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
Salt To taste

Procedure

  • Boil water until hot, add rice and allow to parboil. Then drain
  • Prepare pepper sauce
    • Pour vegetable oil into pot
    • Add sliced onions and stir fry
    • Put pepper blend (mix of scotch bonnets i.e atarodo, tomatoes, red bell peppers i.e tatashe) and tomato puree, then stir
    • Add protein stock (chicken/beef) and allow to simmer for 5 minutes
    • Add curry, thyme, buillon cube and salt to taste
    • Leave to simmer for another 10 minutes
  • Pour parboiled rice into the sauce, and add little water
  • Cover lid and allow to cook for 10-15 minutes
  • Serve while hot with desired protein or veggies *yummy right!*

Extra Tips:

  1. Ensure to wash rice properly to remove excess starch that can cause it to become soggy. 3-4 rinses until water is clear is advised
  2. Stir mixture with wooden spoon to prevent burning
  3. Jollof rice requires steam to cook instead of boiling. Hence, use less water and low heat to prevent burning
  4. Add a little butter to achieve that shimmering look.
